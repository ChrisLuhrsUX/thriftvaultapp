A few framing notes before the ideas:

The reseller niche rewards three things on photo carousels: (1) a specific dollar number or specific brand on slide 1, (2) a "would you have grabbed this?" identity test embedded in the structure, and (3) a payoff slide that resolves uncertainty (resale comp, sold listing screenshot, or in your case, the app result). Your existing 2-photo "item + app screenshot" format is on the right track but it's missing the identity test in the middle — that's the hook layer most of these ideas add.

The shift across all 10 is from "look what the app found" (product-led) to "would you have flipped this?" (audience-led). The app becomes the answer to a question the viewer is already asking in their head by slide 2.

## Idea 1: "Goodwill walk-by test"

Why this works for ThriftVault audience: Resellers and aspiring resellers compulsively second-guess what they pass up at the thrift store — the regret loop is the single strongest emotional driver in the niche. Aesthetic thrifters don't engage with this because they're shopping for outfits, not inventory.

Builds on / replaces from my account: This is the natural replacement for "Scan of the day." Same scan-payoff structure, but the hook reframes from "look at today's scan" (which has plateaued because viewers have no reason to care about *your* scan) to "would you have walked past this?" (which forces the viewer to commit to an answer before the reveal).

Slide-by-slide:
- Slide 1 (hook): Photo of the item on a thrift shelf, ambient/messy, shot from a customer's POV. Text: "Would you have walked past this?"
- Slide 2: Same item picked up, held closer, tag visible. Text: "I almost did."
- Slide 3 (payoff / app screenshot): ThriftVault result showing estimated resale. Text: "$[X] resale. Glad I scanned."

Caption (under 150 chars): walked past this twice before I scanned it. resellers — would you have grabbed it?

Hashtag set: #thriftflip #reseller #depop #poshmark #thriftfinds #sidehustle #thrifting

Music cue: Search "thrift store walking" or pick whatever low-stakes lo-fi/curious-vibe sound is currently trending on reseller carousels. Avoid hyped/dramatic sounds — they cue aesthetic-thrifter algo.

Proof: Format-level pattern, not a specific URL. The "POV: you walked past this at Goodwill" hook is a durable reseller-niche structure.

Why it stops the scroll: Identity callout + commitment device. Viewer can't help mentally answering "yes/no" before swiping.

Risk: If the item is too obviously valuable (vintage Coach, designer label visible), the "would you have walked past" question loses tension — viewers know the answer. Pick items that look mid until the scan reveals otherwise.

## Posting cadence

Don't drop one per day. The reseller niche on TikTok rewards format consistency over format variety because the algo needs 3-5 posts in the same format to figure out who to push it to. If you rotate through all 10 ideas at one-per-day, you'll dilute that signal and each post will be re-discovered cold.

Recommended cadence: pick 3 anchor formats from the top 5 (I'd suggest Idea 1, Idea 8, and Idea 6) and run each as a mini-series of 4-5 posts over two weeks before evaluating. That's roughly one post per day, but only three distinct formats. The other seven ideas stay in the bench and get rotated in after you've identified which of the three anchors hit hardest — at that point you double down on the winner and replace the weakest with a fresh idea from the bench.

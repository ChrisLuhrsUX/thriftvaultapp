### 13c. Tiffany Studios Authenticated Lamp
**Current: $500–$5,000+ | Verdict: CRITICALLY INCORRECT**

No authenticated Tiffany Studios lamp sells for $500. Entry-level auction results for simple geometric patterns start at $4,000–$10,000. Popular patterns (Peony, Dragonfly, Wisteria) are $15,000–$100,000+. The Sotheby's record (Dec 2025): $4.4M for a Magnolia floor lamp. Oriental Poppy sold $1.14M (2025). A $500 authenticated Tiffany Studios piece does not exist in any contemporary market.

The current band likely conflates Tiffany Studios (the historic NY company, 1880–1933) with Tiffany-style reproductions. This is a serious error — if the AI routes a reproduction through this tier, it overprices by 10–20×; if it routes an authentic piece through the reproduction tier, it underprices by 100–1,000×.

**Recommendation:** Correct band to `$4,000–$50,000 (entry-to-mid); rare/major patterns $50,000–$500,000+`. Add explicit disambiguation note: "Tiffany Studios = 1880–1933 New York; always recommend professional appraisal; virtually no authentic Studios lamp appears at thrift prices."

Sources: [Fontaine's Auction price guide](https://www.fontainesauction.com/price-guide-for-tiffany-studios-lamps/) · [DeckTok Tiffany lamp values](https://decktok.com/blogs/smart-lighting-guides/tiffany-lamp-values) · [Artnet Sotheby's Magnolia](https://news.artnet.com/market/tiffany-magnolia-lamp-sothebys-sale-2728111)
